Design Research & Innovation Lead

I turn uncertainty into decisions — through research, prototypes, and strategic design.

I work where innovation is still uncertain. I explore, structure, and materialize opportunities around early-stage and emerging topics, using design to uncover new use cases, make hypotheses tangible, accelerate decision-making, and prepare the path toward industrialization.

My role is to transform these emerging dynamics into concrete projects, align stakeholders from diverse backgrounds and cultures, and help shape hybrid solutions with strong impact.

Case studies

Method

A four-step approach

01

Design

My expertise in designing technological solutions was built at the crossroads of design, applied research, and technological innovation, particularly through projects carried out at the CEA. It also draws on my earlier experience in industrial design, more focused on the design of objects and manufactured products, which shaped my sensitivity to usage, user experience, and product materialization.

See case studies: 01 · 02 · 03

02

Equip

My expertise in designing business and digital tools is rooted in complex contexts where data, systems, and usage need to be articulated to support operational activity. It involves translating business needs, technical models, or complex data into understandable, actionable devices that improve coordination, situational understanding, and decision-making. Beyond interface design, this approach also helps embody a product vision and make future uses tangible within technological contexts that are still evolving.

See case study: 04

03

Evaluate

My expertise in evaluating technological devices through their actual use rests on the ability to complement a technical approach with an understanding of operational realities and field practices. Beyond a technology's performance, this approach aims to analyze its ability to meet real business needs, fit into a given environment, and create value for users.

It relies on phases of experimentation, observation, and inquiry that help identify emerging uses, points of friction, and the conditions for a device's adoption. Design then becomes a lever for connecting technology, context, and usage in order to guide design decisions, among other things.

See case study: 05

04

Advocate

My expertise lies in making complex topics intelligible in order to facilitate decision-making and stakeholder engagement. Through design, I create devices that translate scientific, industrial, or forward-looking issues into accessible, unifying experiences. My goal is to create the conditions for shared understanding, fuel the debate, and support the adoption of major energy, technological, and societal transformations.
